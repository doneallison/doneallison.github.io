About

"...in this little world of studied carnal moneymaking he could find no true province of operation for a spirit essentially gentle and contemplative."

Lawrence Durrell, The Alexandria Quartet

This blog starts with two questions and two hypotheses.

The two questions are:

  • What would I do if money were no object?
  • How can I ensure that money is no object?

And the two hypotheses are:

  • I would think and write about philosophical and technical topics if money were no object; and
  • Entrepreneurship is a viable and sustainable way for me (specifically) to make a living.

I created the concept of monkstrapping—a combination of "monk" and "bootstrapping"—to encompass the two hypotheses in a single word. I don't know exactly how monkstrapping works yet; indeed, I don't even know that it works yet. The purpose of this blog is to find out.

Accordingly, some posts will be about philosophical and technical topics, while others will be about my efforts to start a business. Over time, I hope to learn whether I enjoy writing about philosophy and technology and whether entrepreneurship could fund that lifestyle.

If you have somehow stumbled across this blog, you may be wondering what's in it for you. To be honest, the answer is probably "very little." I'm writing this blog mainly to benefit from accountability to a (probably imagined) audience and also to build a notebook of sorts to see how my thinking evolves over time. Still, you may find an idea or two that you find interesting, be inspired to start your own business, or come up with an idea that helps a business you already have. I hope that ends up being the case!
